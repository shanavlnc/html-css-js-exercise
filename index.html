First Try | Project 1 Details

For the first series of edits that I planned to create, which will include at least three edits per drama, I chose 'Queen of Tears,' a drama that has gained significant attention in 2024. This project features the song 'Where Do We Go Now' by Gracie Abrams to edit a significant scene where the main characters, Hong Haein and Baek Hyunwoo, were about to emotionally reunite and address past issues.

I selected this project to explore how aligning lyrics with visuals can enhance the emotional depth of a scene. By syncing the song’s lyrics with key moments from the drama, I aimed to strengthen the narrative and connect with the audience on a deeper level. Using Adobe Premiere Pro and After Effects, I applied editing techniques and visual effects to create a compelling narrative. This project demonstrates my ability to integrate music with visuals effectively and showcases my skills in video editing and storytelling.

Lyrical and Cinematic Parallels in Editing | Project 2 Details

In this project, I aimed to develop my video editing skills by enhancing the visual quality of my edits, specifically focusing on making the text move fluidly with the characters and refining the timing of text effects like fade-ins and fade-outs, such as using the "fade up words" effect.

For the edit, I used Taylor Swift's "I Wish You Would," which perfectly complements the emotional and complex relationship between the main characters of "Queen of Tears." The song's lyrics, particularly the lines “I wish you would come back… I wish you knew that… I miss you too much to be mad anymore,” resonate with the drama’s exploration of regret, longing, and unresolved feelings between Hong Haein and Baek Hyunwoo. I also highlighted cinematic parallels between different episodes, using the lyrics to mirror the characters' emotions as they navigate the ups and downs of their relationship.

Parallels in Pain and Healing | Project 3 Details

For the final edit in my "Queen of Tears" series, I focused on capturing the emotional depth of the characters' journey by using the song "She's in the Rain" by The Rose. The lyrics, particularly the lines "In my memory your traces spread" and "It's better to be held than holding on," perfectly showcases the contrast between the moments when the characters were on the brink of divorce and when they finally found happiness together.

In this edit, I tried to show the parallels between their struggles and their eventual healing. I selected scenes that visually aligned with the song's themes, such as the rain-soaked moments that symbolize both their pain and their eventual emotional healing at the end of the drama. The editing process involved syncing the lyrics with key scenes to emphasize the characters' internal battles and their journey towards reconciliation.

I paid close attention to transitions and visual effects to enhance the storytelling of my edit. For example, I used slow-motion effects during the bridge to highlight the weight of their emotions, and I tried to make the coloring brighter in the "happy" scenes to show the difference from how they were before.
